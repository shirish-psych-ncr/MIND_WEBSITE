Before your visit

A first consultation is meant to reduce confusion, not add to it

Many people arrive worried about being rushed, judged, or pushed into treatment too quickly. This page is here to make the experience clearer before you walk in.

Before your visit

Book by form, phone, or WhatsApp. If you are unsure which route fits, the clinic can help you decide.
No special preparation is needed. If you already have prescriptions or reports, you may bring them, but they are optional.
Please allow around 45 minutes for a first consultation so the discussion does not feel rushed.
Question people often ask: “Do I need a diagnosis before I book?” No. You can start with what you are experiencing.

During your consultation

The conversation usually begins with what has been troubling you lately and how long it has been affecting daily life.
You may be asked about patterns in sleep, mood, concentration, stress, family context, and past treatment.
For children, the discussion may include development, speech, school concerns, routines, and parent observations.
The aim is to understand the pattern clearly and explain the next steps in plain language.
Question people often ask: “Will I definitely be given medicine?” Not always. Medication is considered when appropriate, alongside practical and therapeutic options.

After the visit

You should leave with more clarity than you came in with

You will usually be given a clear impression of the concern, what needs attention first, and whether follow-up is recommended.
If medicine is part of the plan, the reasons, benefits, and likely concerns should be discussed clearly.
If therapy, routines, parent guidance, or further assessment are more useful, those steps can be prioritised.
