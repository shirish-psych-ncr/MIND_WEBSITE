The sleep–anxiety cycle

Anxiety disrupts sleep. Sleep loss increases anxiety. This bidirectional loop can escalate quietly unless interrupted at both levels.

How the loop works

  1. Trigger: Stress activates fight–flight (cortisol, adrenaline).
  2. Night arousal: Racing thoughts + body activation disrupt sleep.
  3. Sleep anxiety: Worry about sleep becomes a second problem.
  4. Next-day sensitivity: Emotional reactivity increases, control decreases.

Biological layer

  • Amygdala: becomes hyper-reactive to threat
  • Prefrontal cortex: reduced emotional regulation
  • Cortisol: remains elevated at night → hypervigilance

Core strategies (high reliability)

  • Stimulus control: leave bed if awake ~15–20 min
  • Scheduled worry time: offload thoughts earlier
  • Consistent wake time: stabilises rhythm
  • Reduce stimulation: caffeine, screens, late work

Clinical approach (CBT-I)

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy for Insomnia targets both behaviour and cognition:

  • Sleep restriction: align time in bed with actual sleep
  • Stimulus control: rebuild bed–sleep association
  • Cognitive restructuring: reduce catastrophic thinking
  • Paradoxical intention: remove “pressure to sleep”

Cognitive “loop breakers”

  • Backward counting (high effort)
  • Alphabet/category listing
  • Cognitive shuffling (random imagery)
  • Structured journaling before bed

Physiological regulation

  • Slow breathing (longer exhale)
  • Muscle tension → release cycles
  • Cooling face or forehead
  • Light physical movement to discharge arousal

Distress tolerance (DBT layer)

  • Temperature change (cooling face/body)
  • Intense activity (short bursts)
  • Paced breathing
  • Radical acceptance of being awake

Additional strategies (variable effectiveness)

  • Audio distraction (low-interest content)
  • Position change (break bed association)
  • Environmental shifts (lighting, room change)
  • Non-engaging reading

When to seek help

  • Persistent loop (>2–3 weeks)
  • Night-time anxiety dominates
  • Sleep feels effortful or unpredictable
