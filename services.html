Before You Begin

Most people start because something feels harder than it should, not because they already have a diagnosis. These service areas are here to make the next step clearer.

Book Consultation How It Works
Mind Grace Neuropsychiatric Clinic

Adult Psychiatry Services

Expert care for anxiety, depression, ADHD, sleep concerns, and more

Psychiatric Consultation

You might start here if you are not fully sure what is going on but want a structured first conversation. Often useful for anxiety, mood changes, sleep concerns, attention problems, or overlapping symptoms.

Book Consultation Learn more about our services

Medication Review

Useful if you are already on medication, adjusting to treatment, or trying to understand whether the current plan still fits. Follow-up visits focus on response, side effects, daily functioning, and practical next steps.

Book Follow-up

Anxiety and Panic Care

You might relate to this if there is constant worry, panic, overthinking, physical tension, or avoidance. Support may include explanation, coping strategy planning, therapy guidance, and medication when appropriate.

Get Help See Symptoms

Mood Support

You might start here if there is loss of interest, heaviness, irritability, exhaustion, or difficulty functioning. The first step is understanding the pattern and daily impact before deciding what kind of treatment fits best.

Get Help See Symptoms

Adult ADHD Assessment

You might relate to this if concentration, organisation, task-starting, forgetfulness, or overwhelm have been long-standing problems. Assessment can include pattern review, practical support, and discussion of treatment options.

Book Assessment Read Articles
Aasha Early Intervention

Child Development & Parent Support

Specialized care for speech, occupational therapy, and parent guidance

Speech and Language Support

Useful when talking seems delayed, unclear, inconsistent, or harder than expected for age. Early support is often most helpful when communication is affecting learning, interaction, or confidence.

Book Child Consultation

Occupational Therapy

Useful for sensory regulation, coordination, attention, self-help tasks, and daily participation concerns. Parents often notice these concerns in routines, play, school transitions, or behaviour.

Book Child Consultation

Parent Guidance

Useful when families need practical ways to understand behaviour, routines, school demands, or communication patterns. This often works best when parents, caregivers, and therapists are aligned around one clear plan.

Book Parent Session Read Articles
