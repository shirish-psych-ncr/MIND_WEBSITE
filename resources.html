Mental health tools , reliable and practical

These tools can support grounding, calming, and focus. They are meant to sit alongside professional care, not replace it.

Tools for Mental Well-Being

Guided Breathing

A simple way to calm the body when anxiety, overthinking, or restlessness builds up. Follow visual cues to regulate your breath.

Start Exercise

Lateral Grounding

Keep your head still. Follow the moving light with your eyes only. Helps reduce acute anxiety through bilateral stimulation.

Start Session

Fractal Grounding

Tap to explode a fractal. Hold and move to swirl. Match your breath to the glow. Visual meditation for focus and calm.

Launch Tool

River of Release

Type a worry, watch it drift away on a leaf. A cognitive defusion exercise to help externalize and release anxious thoughts.

Release a Worry

Butterfly Tapper

Alternate tapping with bilateral stimulation. Follow the comet, tap when ready. Helps process emotional logjams.

Begin Tapping

Horizon Scan

Use smooth eye movements across an infinite horizon to lessen distress and expand your visual field during panic.

Start Exercise

When to seek professional help

  • Symptoms lasting weeks or getting worse
  • Sleep, appetite, or energy significantly affected
  • Difficulty functioning at work, school, or home
  • Frequent distress, panic, or emotional overwhelm
  • Concerns about safety, self-harm, or substance use

If you're unsure , that itself is a valid reason to consult a mental health professional.
