In Crisis? Read This First

You're not alone — but this needs immediate support

If you or someone near you is in immediate danger, at risk of self-harm, experiencing a severe mental health crisis, or has taken an overdose — please act now.

Call emergency services immediately:
112

Or go to the nearest hospital emergency room.

What you can do right now

Immediate actions to keep yourself or others safe

Stay Together

Do not leave the person alone. Your presence matters more than perfect words.

Remove Dangers

Safely remove access to medications, sharp objects, or anything harmful.

Speak Calmly

Use a gentle voice. Listen without judgment. Reassure them help is coming.

Call for Help

Reach out to emergency services or a 24/7 helpline immediately. Keep trying if lines are busy.

24/7 Mental Health Helplines (India)

Free, confidential support available any time, day or night

Kiran Mental Health Helpline

1800-599-0019

Government of India initiative. Available in multiple languages.

Vandrevala Foundation

9999 666 555

24/7 crisis intervention and suicide prevention support.

Childline

1098

For children in distress, abuse, or harm. Free and confidential.

Emergency Services

112

Single emergency number for police, fire, and medical assistance.

If one line is busy, try another. Keep trying.

These helplines are staffed by trained counselors who can provide immediate support and guidance.
