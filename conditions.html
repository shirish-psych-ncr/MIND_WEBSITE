Condition-first guidance

You can begin with the pattern you are noticing, even if you do not know its name

These are some of the concerns people commonly ask about. If one of them sounds familiar, it may help you choose a more useful starting point.

For adults

Anxiety and panic

You might relate to this if worry feels constant, your body stays tense, panic comes in waves, or sleep gets disrupted by racing thoughts.

A first consultation can help separate stress, panic, and longer-standing anxiety patterns.

See related care

Low mood and depression

You might relate to this if you feel emotionally heavy, withdrawn, numb, unmotivated, or unable to recover your usual energy.

Low mood may look different across work, relationships, sleep, appetite, and concentration.

See related care

Adult ADHD and focus concerns

You might relate to this if tasks feel hard to begin, concentration slips easily, or organisation has always taken more effort than it should.

Assessment can help distinguish ADHD-like patterns from stress, burnout, or sleep-related difficulty.

See related care

Other adult concerns

Sleep concerns

Trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking unrefreshed can be part of a broader anxiety, mood, or routine pattern.

Stress and burnout

Feeling depleted, detached, and unable to recover from work or family strain may need more than “just rest.”

Obsessions, intrusive thoughts, or rituals

If thoughts feel repetitive, unwanted, and difficult to let go of, a structured review can help clarify what is happening.

For children and development

Autism and developmental differences

You might notice concerns around eye contact, interaction, repetitive patterns, flexibility, communication, or everyday regulation.

Early understanding can make support more focused and less overwhelming for families.

See child support

Speech and language delay

You might notice late talking, unclear speech, difficulty expressing needs, or trouble following language expected for age.

A first conversation can help decide whether speech support, developmental review, or school guidance is more urgent.

See child support

If you are still unsure

It is common for several concerns to overlap. You do not need to sort them perfectly before reaching out.
